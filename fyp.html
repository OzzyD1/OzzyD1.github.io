Synapse: Engineered Perception

by Ozzy Domarkas - 20068200

Exploring the Ethical and Psychological Implications of Perception Manipulation Through Brain-Computer Interfaces in an Interactive Live-Action Narrative

Synapse is a thought-provoking interactive live-action experience that explores the dangers of corporate control, transhumanism, and the ethical dilemmas surrounding brain-computer interfaces (BCIs). Set in a near-future dystopia, the story follows The Programmer, an overworked developer who is pressured into using a brain-enhancing device to maintain his productivity. As he delves deeper into the technology, he faces increasing side effects and unsettling discoveries, forcing him to make critical choices that shape his fate.

This narrative-driven project is designed as an interactive experience where the viewer actively participates in The Programmerâ€™s decisions. The choices made by the audience dictate whether he uncovers the truth, becomes consumed by the system, or finds a way to break free.
