Visual storytelling comes naturally to me, and video production has become one of my strongest creative outlets. Alongside producing story-driven films that explore narrative and emotion, I have also created a wide range of corporate videos, combining creativity with clear communication. My projects reflect both artistic vision and technical expertise, with a focus on delivering engaging, professional results.

A Day in the Life of Andrew - Expectation Vs Reality

I led this creative project as part of a four-person team. After one member drafted the script, I refined it to improve flow and narrative clarity. I took full responsibility for editing and visual effects in Adobe After Effects, leveraging my strongest skills to shape the final product. In addition, I directed and supported the other two team members during acting and filming, ensuring consistency in performance and visual style.

Kidnapping

I wrote and directed this action sequence, deliberately minimising cuts to capture the intensity of single-take action scenes. Despite time constraints and limited equipment, I was able to achieve the intended style and was satisfied with the final result.

Takedown

This was one of my first video projects where I centred the storytelling on dialogue and built tension through a dramatic reveal at the end. The narrative follows a detective confronting a former mob boss turned CEO, with the conversation serving as a psychological duel. My focus was on pacing, character interaction, and subtle visual cues to keep the audience engaged in a two-character dialogue-driven scene.

Logistics

This was my most ambitious After Effects project to date, where I designed a dynamic world map animation to visualise the logistics of shipments from China to Europe. The piece combined motion graphics, data visualisation, and stylistic design choices to present global trade routes in the context of the current climate
